Business task:

Use Cyclistic’s historical trip data to identify and compare the trends between the behavior of casual riders and that of member riders over a period of a year, and use your insights to drive business decisions.

The data:

Location and licensing:

The dataset is composed with twelve csv files representing the twelve most recent months and is located here . It has been made available by Motivate International Inc. under this license.

reliability:

The data is internal primary data which makes it not only credible, but also reliable. It contains data about “casual and member rides” and their duration, which will be used in the analysis phase of the project.

Bias:

Although the majority of Cyclistic riders opt for the traditional bikes, the data the data includes all riders.

Problems with the data:

All the csv files in the dataset have missing values, and the file for the month of December 2020 has misplaced column names.

Data transformation:

Daily number of rides:

Over all, the number of member rides on a given day of the week is fairly consistent, oscillating between 330000 and 400000. which suggests that members are a reliable customer base that continuously puts its trust in Cyclistic. Casual rides on the other hand, come out on specific days, being Saturday, Sunday, and Monday. In fact, over all, the number of casual riders is only higher than the number member riders on Sunday and Monday.

Recomendations:

  • Build an advertising campaign around “casual rides” high traffic days, namely Saturday, Sunday and Monday.
  • The campaign should focus on two points:
    • The Value of the membership.
    • The value of the short ride.

Show casual riders that they can save money by using Cyclistic on week days and not only the weekends.

  • Establish a metric that would assess how well a campaign is performing. A suggested metric would be comparing the number of casual riders to the number of member riders on a given weekend. The goal being that the number of casual riders will be less than 50% of the over all number of riders.
